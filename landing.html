Sources & citations
Local anaesthetic toxicity & maximum doses
AAGBI Safety Guideline — Management of Severe Local Anaesthetic Toxicity ↗
Association of Anaesthetists of Great Britain & Ireland, 2010. Primary source for maximum mg/kg dose limits used throughout LASTCalc.
El-Boghdadly K et al. — Local anaesthetic systemic toxicity: current perspectives ↗
Local and Regional Anesthesia, 2018; 11: 35–44. doi:10.2147/LRA.S154512. Fractional dose mixture rationale and clinical risk stratification.
AFOi topical lidocaine ceiling (9 mg/kg)
DAS Guidelines for awake tracheal intubation in adults ↗
Difficult Airway Society, Anaesthesia 2020; 75: 509–528. Source for 9 mg/kg topical lidocaine ceiling used in AFOi calculations.
Williams KA, Woodall NM et al. — Combined nebulisation and spray-as-you-go topical local anaesthesia ↗
BJA 2005; 95(4): 549–553. Demonstrated 9 mg/kg topical lidocaine does not produce toxic plasma concentrations; basis for AFOi ceiling.
Obesity-adjusted dosing
Janmahasatian M et al. — Quantification of lean bodyweight ↗
Clinical Pharmacokinetics 2005; 44(10): 1051–1065. doi:10.2165/00003088-200544100-00004. Formula used to calculate lean body weight (LBW) when BMI ≥ 30.
Devine BJ — Ideal Body Weight formula (ClinCalc reference) ↗
Original: Drug Intelligence & Clinical Pharmacy 1974; 8: 650–655 (pre-digital, no direct URL). ClinCalc provides the validated formula and clinical context used for IBW calculation in LASTCalc.
Paediatric dosing
BNF for Children — Local anaesthetics ↗
British National Formulary for Children (current edition). Age-banded maximum dose ceilings for neonates, infants, and children.
APAGBI — Association of Paediatric Anaesthetists of Great Britain & Ireland ↗
Paediatric anaesthesia dose ceiling context drawn from APAGBI guidance. No single standalone LA guideline is publicly indexed; refer to the APAGBI website for current publications and safety statements.
Nerve block volumes & drug selection
NYSORA — Clinical Pharmacology of Local Anesthetics ↗
Hadzic A (ed.). Volumes, concentrations, and expected durations for individual nerve blocks; maximum dose guidance by agent.
Bardsley H et al. — Cardiac and CNS toxicity of levobupivacaine: strengths of evidence for advantage over bupivacaine ↗
Drug Safety 2002; 25(3): 153–163. Evidence basis for preferring levobupivacaine at vascular-adjacent blocks where inadvertent IV injection is a risk.

Dose references. Maximum doses are derived from AAGBI, DAS, and standard published anaesthetic references. Local formularies and departmental protocols may differ — always verify doses against your own institution's guidance before administering any drug.

Patient safety. No calculator can account for all individual patient factors. Clinical decisions must be made by a qualified practitioner who has assessed the patient directly. If in doubt, seek senior advice.
